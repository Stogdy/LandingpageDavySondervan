Why did I make Blackbeard?

Blackbeard (Marshall D. Teach) was created by Eiichiro Oda to serve as a dark counterpart to Monkey D. Luffy in One Piece. Oda designed him to embody traits like ambition, betrayal, and an unrelenting hunger for power, contrasting Luffy's freedom-loving and loyal nature.

Who is Blackbeard?

Blackbeard, also known as Marshall D. Teach, is a character from One Piece created by Eiichiro Oda. He is the captain of the Blackbeard Pirates and one of the most notorious villains in the series. Teach is a former member of the Whitebeard Pirates who betrayed his crew to obtain the powerful Yami Yami no Mi, a Logia-type Devil Fruit that allows him to control darkness. Later, he also acquired the Gura Gura no Mi, giving him the power to create earthquakes. Ambitious and ruthless, Blackbeard aims to become the Pirate King by manipulating events and pursuing ultimate power.
